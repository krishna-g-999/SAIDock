About SAIDock

SAIDock is an automated drug-target surface analysis and molecular docking platform developed as part of the SAI-Net (Structure-Activity Intelligence Network) computational neuropharmacology framework at Sri Sathya Sai Institute of Higher Learning, Puttaparthi.

The platform integrates multi-pocket detection, AutoDock Vina docking, protein-ligand interaction profiling, ADMET prediction, and a novel ML-based Drug-Target Suitability Score (DTSS) to profile candidate therapeutic compounds against NDD-relevant protein targets.

Pipeline Modules

  • [1/8] Target Preparation — PDB download, chain extraction, missing residue repair, PDBQT generation
  • [2/8] Pocket Detection — fpocket Voronoi; composite druggability scoring; top-5 pockets selected
  • [3/8] Ligand Preparation — SMILES → 3D (ETKDG + MMFF94), pH 7.4 protonation, PDBQT conversion
  • [4/8] Docking — AutoDock Vina 1.2, exhaustiveness=16, multi-pocket screening
  • [5/8] Interactions — ProLIF H-bond, π-stacking, hydrophobic contacts; distance fallback
  • [6/8] ADMET — QED, LogP, TPSA, Lipinski, BBB penetration, GI absorption (RDKit)
  • [7/8] ML + DTSS — Per-target Random Forest (n=300, 2063-dim Morgan+physicochemical fingerprint); adaptive DTSS with applicability domain flagging
  • [8/8] Report — HTML surface report + JSON export with full DTSS component breakdown

DTSS Score

The Drug-Target Suitability Score (DTSS) is a composite [0,1] metric combining five evidence streams with adaptive weights:

  • ΔG_norm × 0.35–0.45 — Normalised Vina docking free energy
  • ADMET × 0.20 — Drug-likeness and safety profile
  • ML × 0.10–0.20 — Target-specific bioactivity prediction (ChEMBL-trained RF)
  • Pocket druggability × 0.15 — fpocket composite score
  • Literature prior × 0.10 — ChEMBL pChEMBL converted to [0,1]
  • ≥ 0.65 — Good to Excellent: strong multi-evidence binding support
  • 0.50–0.65 — Moderate: promising, consider structural optimisation
  • 0.35–0.50 — Weak: limited evidence, interpret with caution
